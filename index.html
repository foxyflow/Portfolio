Moving down to Educational Experience and why I'm not seeking web development from frameworks.

From my CS degree, I was fortunate to learn ASP.NET C#, ReactJs, Node, REST, APIs, Http requests, JSON; with databases, PHP, SQL, WAMP, SQL Server, NoSQL, and Oracle. The knowledge gained gave me a good understanding of web development and its dependences. And although the world depends on JavaScript frameworks now, they don't have a solid foundation for tomorrow. Future web apps will be written in languages with less dependences such as the C family: C, Zig, C++, Python, or C# or a similar wrapper for frontend and just get complied to web-assembly. -- When a JS framework breaks from a dependency update, it becomes frustrating having to fix something from not knowing my website is down. This portfolio is written in JS, CSS, HTML and only relies on the Github server, it has lasted since university days because I didnot use a framework, but its still built on sand -- good enough to add projects or links for job hunting.

Summary, C family projects have a solid foundation, when I spend free time programming, I'm in that arena.
